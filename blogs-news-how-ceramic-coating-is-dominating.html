How Ceramic Coating is Dominating the Detailing Industry

How Ceramic Coating is Dominating the Detailing Industry

Posted by ShineArmor on October 22nd 2018

Classic methods of car detailing are slowly going out of fashion. Wax or sealant protects your car for a limited period, but in recent years, ceramic coating has revolutionized the detailing industry.

Using nanotechnology, ceramic coatings chemically bond with the paintwork to form a new layer of protection. Invisible to the naked eye, this molecular coating is thinner than a human hair, yet the protection it provides is unrivalled.

Superior Benefits of Ceramic Coatings:

✅ Water Repellent

  • Hydrophobic, or water repellent properties are the first line of defence. The ceramic layer instantly repels water, causing it to bead and simply roll off.
  • Mud and dirt can no longer adhere to the car surface, making it much easier to clean.

✅ UV Damage Protection

  • A combination of heat and oxygen cause oxidation of your car’s paintwork. While your engine can make the bonnet hot, long-term exposure to the sun is the biggest threat.  
  • Regular waxing used to be the preferred choice. However, ceramic coatings’ protection prevents the sun’s UV rays from fading the paintwork.

✅ Long Lasting

  • Waxing your car typically lasts between 8-12 weeks, before you need to do it all over again. Ceramic coatings last up to 3 times longer.

✅ Chemical Resistant

  • Acidic fluids and winter salts accelerate damage on your car’s paintwork. The chemical resistance of a ceramic coating prevents bird droppings and bug splatter corroding into the paintwork.

✅ Glossy, Shiny Finish

  • The physical appearance of a glossy, shiny car is the most noticeable benefit. A ceramic coating transforms your car into a self-cleaning machine.

How much should it cost?

For this quality of protection, there’s always a cost involved. Taking your car to a detailer for a ceramic coating, can cost anywhere between $500-$3000. The size, condition and how often the car is driven, are all factors considered when calculating the price.

Like all good things, ceramic coatings don’t last forever. Most detailers recommend a touch up at least every 9 months, which will set you back a few hundred dollars each time.

Not to mention the expense and hassle of washing your car every two weeks, to keep its hydrophobic properties intact. No doubt, your detailer will recommend expensive specialized products for home use, to maintain the glossy look.
