How To Get Rid Of Hyperpigmentation

You’ve just run out of your favorite concealer, and you realize you have less than an hour before your big Zoom call. Now everyone is going to see the dark blotches on your face, neck, or chest. Wouldn’t it be great if there was a way to make those dark spots fade away for good?

 

We’re not talking about super expensive treatments from dermatologists either. While those prescriptions and treatments may work fine for some people, we’d like to recommend some all-natural methods that won’t break the bank.

 

What Is Hyperpigmentation? 

 

Hyperpigmentation can affect anyone, and it’s characterized by patches of skin which become darker in color than the skin around them. Because of a buildup of melanin, the skin’s natural hue darkens, leaving you with unwanted dark spots. Many people refer to these hyperpigmentation spots as sunspots, liver spots, age spots, or melasma.

 

What Causes Hyperpigmentation?

 

So, what causes these dark spots that randomly appear? Many times, it can result from too much sun exposure, acne breakouts, hormonal imbalances, genetics, or other trauma your skin may have sustained. For those who can’t resist the urge to pick at their face, this can result in scarring or hyperpigmentation as well.

 

Usually the main culprit that leads to hyperpigmentation is sun exposure. The sun’s rays can cause dark spots, also known as sunspots, to form very easily on your skin. While we all like to soak up a little vitamin D, unfortunately, if we leave our skin unprotected, it can lead to the development of unwelcomed sun spots.

 

It’s important to know that when you venture out in the sun, the rays you’re soaking up increase the production of melanin in your body. This isn’t necessarily a bad thing since melanin can help protect your skin from damaging UV rays. 


The problem is that you can’t over do it. Excessive exposure to sunlight can lead to the development of discolorations, and once formed, additional run-ins with the sun only make your hyperpigmentation worse.

 

What Can You Do? 

 

If you weren’t as careful about sun exposure when you were a kid, or picked at your acne, or have genetically inherited hyperpigmentation, it might feel like there’s nothing you can do except try to frantically cover up your dark spots. 


If you’ve been dealing with this for a while, it may seem like it will never go away, but it doesn’t have to be that way. There are ways to reduce your chances of acquiring more dark spots and to help fade the ones you have. 

 

Prevention 

 

  • Sunscreen

 

Clearly, it’s best to avoid hyperpigmentation in the first place. The best first step toward preventing sunspots from appearing is to protect yourself with sunscreen. It’s a quick, easy, and safe solution that you can add to your daily routine. No matter how long you plan to be outdoors, it’s always best to spray or rub on some sunscreen before leaving the house. 

 

  • Protective Layers 

 

Regardless of your skin tone, hyperpigmentation can affect anyone. So, don’t fall for the misconception that darker skin is immune to sun damage. It’s best to protect your skin in every way you can. Carrying extra layers of clothing, sunglasses, and even a hat or visor to shield your face are great ways to protect the beautiful skin Mother Nature gave you throughout the day.

 

  • Don’t Pick!

You can also avoid creating areas of hyperpigmentation by refraining from picking at or popping pimples, scratching at bug bites, or creating any type of open wounds that could lead to scarring.


Often, these scars when exposed to sunlight can turn into hyperpigmentation hot spots, leaving you with unwanted dark spots or blotches that can be difficult to cover up.


  • Hydrate 

Lastly, locking moisture into your skin is another great way to prevent dark spots from wreaking havoc on your skin. Being hydrated helps support healthy, youthful looking skin and decreases your chances of sun damage. So, keep a water bottle close to ward off dry, flaky skin. Keeping your skin hydrated and healthy can do wonders for reducing the likelihood of dark spots popping up when you least expect them.

 

Ways To Reduce Hyperpigmentation 

 

  • Vitamin C

Vitamin C is part of just about every sought-after skin care routine because of how versatile and effective it is. Vitamin C assists in fading those dreaded dark spots. Because of its acidic nature, vitamin C helps to exfoliate the skin, which is fabulous for your overall skin health. This exfoliation removes unwanted dead skin cells from the surface of your skin and allows you to enjoy silky smooth skin that radiates confidence. Bye, bye dark spots.

 

Vitamin C can also brighten and firm for a supple, youthful complexion, reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. 


  • Other Vitamins

Additionally, vitamins A and E are also great for your skin. Vitamin E helps to protect your skin against sun damage, and vitamin A has been shown to reduce the appearance of dark spots. These three vitamins are a recipe for success when it comes to lightening those pesky dark skin spots and restoring your confidence in your radiant skin.


  • Turmeric

Have you ever heard of turmeric? If not, you’re in for a real treat, as turmeric is another all-natural ingredient that works to maintain normal pigmentation in your skin. It can also reduce the appearance of dark spots and scarring because it boosts your skin’s ability to protect itself from the sun’s harmful rays. 

 

Curcumin, an active ingredient of turmeric, also assists by acting as a soothing agent for your skin and helping reduce the production of melanin. This can even out your skin tone and lighten dark spots. This can even help lighten scars left over from acne episodes. Turmeric powder can be mixed together with lots of other active ingredients and made into a face mask to help fade your current scars and dark spots.


  • Aloe Vera 

Aloe vera is another great option when you’re looking to get rid of your hyperpigmentation spots. Aloe vera works to soothe and calm your skin, especially if you are suffering from any sun damage. This is a plant of many talents, as many have found that aloe vera can also lend a hand to help lighten dark spots and reduce the appearance of any hyperpigmentation areas that really bother you.


  •  Clay

And next up, we have a hyperpigmentation preventative player who just may surprise you. Did you know that certain types of clay are used to shrink the appearance of your pores and shed impurities, so you can wash all that gunk down the drain? Although typically recommended for people suffering from acne, clay can actually help lighten the dark spots you are struggling with.

 

All clay face masks are unique, and each one has different ingredients to assist with all different kinds of skincare needs. White clay, also known as kaolin, is the most mild type of clay. It can gently help draw out impurities, hydrate your skin, and lighten the appearance of your dark spots leaving you feeling happy, healthy, and confident. White clay, despite its name, can come in a variety of colors such as white, yellow, red, and pink.

 

You can also opt for yellow clay if you’re craving something for your oily skin but still want to cater to your sensitive skin care needs. If sensitivity is not a huge issue for you and you’re looking to really draw out impurities and lighten the appearance of your dark spots, then you can choose red clay with confidence. And lastly, pink clay is that middle ground if you need a good balance of mildness and absorption to keep your skin looking fabulous.

 

  • Other Ingredients

While on your quest to reduce your hyperpigmentation skin spots, you can also opt for some additional, less traditional home remedies such as licorice extract and apple cider vinegar which are both known to help reduce the appearance of skin discolorations.


Some people have also highly recommended green tea bags , black tea water, and lactic acid (found in milk and buttermilk) to assist in lightening sunspots and other discolorations

 

  • Face Masks

So, how can you combine all of these amazing ingredients into one effective skin care treatment? Face masks just may be the key to getting rid of your hyperpigmentation skin spots. But what kind of face mask should you try? What will yield the best results when it comes to reducing sunspots and revealing your radiant skin?

 

You have many options when it comes to clay facial masks and the benefits that come with them. Face masks can make your life easier and save you money on the makeup you so desperately rely on to help you cover up those pesky spots.

 

Gleamin offers a face mask that incorporates kaolin clay, vitamin C, aloe vera, turmeric, and more. It’s like a one-stop shop for all your hyperpigmentation needs. Imagine not having to constantly worry or be self-conscious about dark spots popping up on your face or body. It would be like a dream come true, right?

 

Most of us have used a face mask before and know they are fairly quick and easy to use. Just think of all the possibilities. You can choose a clay mask that hydrates, exfoliates, and lightens dark unsightly spots on your skin for a smoother, even-looking complexion.

 

In Summary 

 

Hyperpigmentation can be a pain, but it doesn’t have to rule your life. We understand that dark spots can pop up from time to time, but the good news is that you don’t have to settle for letting them settle on your face for good. 


You can take preventative action to lessen the likelihood of these unwanted visitors, but if you do experience some hyperpigmentation, it’s always good to know your options, like the home remedies we’ve discussed. Always explore the ingredients offered in your skincare products and choose what works best for you and your beautiful skin.

 

Take that me time for yourself, and say goodbye to those pesky dark spots you didn’t invite in the first place. Pretty soon you’ll be saying who needs that foundation?
