Can You Get Hair Transplant on Thinning Hair?

July 17, 2020/in Hair Loss 101 /by Nicholas

No one wants to go bald. Who doesn’t want the thick beautiful hair they likely took for granted in high school? Unfortunately, this is simply not in the cards for everyone. Some people begin experiencing thinning hair soon after puberty, while others are seniors when they start to go bald and have a small amount of hair. Either way, baldness is a situation that many must face. Balding can be a problem that has a dramatic, negative, impact on people’s self-image, self-confidence, and overall mental health. For all of these reasons and others, many people have turned to hair transplants as a permanent, attractive solution.

What Is a Hair Transplant?

A hair transplant surgery is a procedure where an experienced doctor manually extracts hair follicles from a “donor” area near the back of your scalp and grafts them onto your bald spots. The revolutionary procedure helps patients grow hair in areas where they thought their hair was gone forever. It doesn’t matter if you’re a man or a woman, hair transplantation can help you deal with hair loss.

The efficiency of a hair transplant depends on the ability and competence of the doctor. Dr. Pak of the NHI Medical is a renowned physician for his artful hair restoration skills. Former engineer, Dr. Jae Pak holds multiple U.S. Patents as well.

What Are the Two Main Types of Hair Transplants?

Can you get a hair transplant on thinning hair? Certainly! Hair transplantation is not just for bald spots. The hair transplant procedure is utilized in multiple conditions ranging from thinning scalp hair and bald areas to non-uniform beard and eyebrow growth. Imagine! You’re on your way to a party while flicking your beautiful voluminous hair, and everyone is awestruck by the change in your look and personality. Sounds amazing! Doesn’t it?

There are two common types of hair transplants that help you gain such confidence. They include:

  • Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE)
  • Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT)

How Are the Two Main Types of Hair Transplant Different?

Follicular Unit Transplant is a process that helps you gain long or short hair. It involves the extraction of a cluster of hair follicles, usually forming a fringe from around your head, and incorporating it into the bald areas. This technique, pioneered by NHI Medicaledical experts, is a renowned method for the best hair growth.

Follicular Unit Excision, on the other hand, is a minimally invasive procedure that helps you gain hair by excising individual hair follicles from the donor site of your body. These hair follicles are then artistically relocated on the recipient site. This type of procedure is ideal for scar revisions, hairline correction, beard, and eyebrow transplantation. While the Follicular Unit Excision does have less spent in terms of recovery time, it does require a longer time spent in the actual operation. A major FUE can take several sessions of 7-hours or more.

If Your Hair Is Thinning, Can You Get a Hair Transplant?

Many people frequently ask, “Can you get hair transplant on thinning hair?” the answer is Yes! You can! Hair thinning is a problem faced by millions of people. Unlike baldness, you don’t lose hair entirely from an area; instead, their number gets fewer and fewer. Many men and women suffer from a lack of confidence due to hair thinning. There are a lot of factors contributing to hair thinning, but only a few are reversible. The long-term solution for this problem lies in hair transplantation.

Professionals at NHI Medical help you achieve your hair goals, giving you a much-deserved confidence boost. You can style your hair as you want and look fabulous. You don’t need hats to hide your head anymore! It is time to let the healthy-looking hair shine!

Who Is a Good Candidate for a Hair Transplant?

Hair transplants have gained popularity worldwide. More and more people with hair problems are opting for this procedure. If you’re wondering what makes a right candidate for hair transplantation, anyone who is concerned about his condition and wants to improve it is eligible. People who opt for a hair transplant suffer from the following problems:

  • Male pattern baldness
  • Receding hairline
  • Hair thinning on the scalp
  • Thin and uneven beard hair
  • Bald spots in eyebrows

Gaining hair on your scalp isn’t a problem anymore! However, there are some medical conditions that raise concerns and make you in-eligible for a hair transplant. Some of them are mentioned below:

  • The active phase of Alopecia Areata
  • Psoriasis
  • Diabetes
  • Coagulation disorders
  • Keloid tendencies
  • Active infection

It is always better to consult your physician and explain everything in detail. Discuss your concerns and let them know if there is any medical condition they’re unaware of.

What Is the Recovery Time for a Hair Transplant?

When going for a hair transplant procedure, it is best to keep your expectations realistic. No new hair will spring out of your head immediately. Remember! All good things take time! After the surgery, you’ll have tenderness in your scalp. Doctors suggest wearing bandages for at least one or two days after you get your transplant.

If you’re wondering how long before you can go to work again, most people recover within five days. If you notice the transplanted hair falling out, don’t panic! They’ll fall out after two- or three-weeks post-surgery and new hair will start growing after a few months. Keep in mind that every single follicle might not grow new hair. But, you’ll be able to see a significant difference.

What About Thinning Hair?

Can you get a transplant on thinning hair? Will the bald spots go away? If you think about any of these questions, know that it is possible via hair transplantation and has a great outcome. The expertise of the doctor hugely matters in determining the result of this type of surgery. So, it is best to opt for quality medical services like those of the NHI Medical.

All in all, hair transplant helps you renew your outlook and gain confidence. The days of hiding your head are over! If you have been suffering from hair thinning or baldness, consult a physician now!
