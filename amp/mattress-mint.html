Premium polyurethane foam crafted in the USA.

Our foam is tested and certified by CertiPUR-US® to meet high standards in physical performance, content, indoor emissions and environmental stewardship.

Two-layer blended foam is designed by, and proprietary to, Tuft & Needle. The support foam layer is a high-density poly-foam. The density is about 1.8 lbs/cubic ft. The bottom layer includes 4 sides of edge support of the same density.

The middle and bottom layers of T&N Adaptive® foam is a very high-density foam that we created to achieve our universally comfortable, cool and responsive properties. The density for the middle layer is about 3.0 lbs/cubic ft. with the top layer being 3.5 lbs/cubic ft.

Our Mint ® Mattress cover is made with a blend of micro polyamide and polyester. It has an ultra soft feel which is amazing to the touch.

Micro Polyamide is commonly used in clothing for men, women and children as an alternative to cotton. It is also known to dry 6-8 times faster than cotton, which helps you sleep cooler, longer.

With durability in mind, we've designed a fabric that will last. Our Mattress cover fabric is pilling and tearing resistant.

Ready to order, but have questions?

  • When will I get my Mint ™ Mattress?

    Your mattress will arrive compressed and packed in a small box within 1-5 business days. Holidays and winter weather may affect shipping times. Setup is easy and the same as our original T&N Mattress, which you can see our co-founder Daehee unpacking here .

  • How do the three layers in the Mint ™ Mattress compare to the two layers in the T&N Mattress?

    Our T&N Mattress has two layers that work together to provide the sleeper the ideal combination of comfort and support. The three layers in the Mint ™ Mattress work together to provide remarkable support that you'll find comfortable immediately as well as throughout the night. We increased the softness of the top layer for better pressure relief, increased the firmness of the bottom support layer (for edge support), and added a middle transitional layer to keep you from "bottoming" out.

  • What does the 10-Year Limited Warranty cover?

    We stand behind our mattresses and offer a 10-year limited warranty to prove it. This warranty is limited to material defects resulting from defective materials or workmanship in the mattress. To read more about our mattress warranty, please click here .

Helpful FAQs

  • How long will it take for my original Tuft & Needle mattress to arrive?

    Once you've placed your order, your original Tuft & Needle Mattress will arrive in about 2-5 business days. We ship via FedEx Ground standard shipping and deliver Monday through Friday, excluding holidays. Upon shipping, we'll send you an email with your FedEx tracking information so you can stay up to date on your shipment. We recommend keeping an eye on your spam folder as well as your inbox since a lot of business emails are directed there automatically! Deliveries to Hawaii and Alaska can take up to 10 business days to arrive. For areas where same-day delivery is available and the order is placed by 3:45 local time, our courier will arrive between 4-9:30 pm. Tracking information is unavailable for these orders.

  • What do you mean by "platform," "slatted," "slats," "foundation," etc.?

    There are different types of frame and foundation options that would work perfectly with our Tuft & Needle mattresses: Platform Bed Frame - Platform bed frames come in a variety of styles, and they either feature horizontal slats (think planks, usually wooden) or a solid platform for the mattress to rest on. Slatted Bed Frame - A slatted bed frame is another way of saying platform frame and gets its name from the characteristic horizontal slats that support the mattress. When shopping for a slatted bed frame, make sure the slats are no more than 5 inches apart. If the gaps in between each slat are too big, a box spring or bunky board may be necessary Slats - Slats are the planks that are usually placed horizontally on a slatted or platform frame. Their purpose is to support the mattress and their function is analogous to that of a box spring. Metal Bed Frame - Sometimes called a Hollywood bed frame, the metal frame is a traditional bed frame that supports a box spring + mattress. For this specific type of a frame, a box spring or bunky board is required. Box Spring - Traditionally featuring a wooden frame and springs, most modern box springs are, in fact, slatted or solid foundations. Their purpose is to provide even support under the mattress, and they work with either a metal bed frame or a platform bed. Solid Foundation - A solid foundation provides the support of a platform frame or slats, though it looks very much like a box spring and is used the same way: metal frame + solid foundation + mattress. Most modern box springs are solid or slatted foundations. You can learn more about a variety of box spring, frame and foundation types on our blog - article linked here .

    View on FAQ page

  • Do the Tuft & Needle mattresses come with a box spring?

    The Tuft & Needle mattresses do not come with a box spring. A box spring is optional to use with our mattresses, but feel free to use one if you prefer the height that it offers. It isn’t necessary to purchase a new one if you already have an existing box spring, either! You should get the same comfort and support either way as long as your box spring is intact and in good shape. The mattresses work great with most frames and foundations, including platform frames, slatted frames, box springs, wooden foundations, adjustable frames, and even the floor. Learn more about the difference between a box spring and a foundation on our blog, here . If you’re in the market for a new box foundation, check out our Basics Box Foundation .
