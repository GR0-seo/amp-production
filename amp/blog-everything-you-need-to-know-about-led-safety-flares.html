Everything You Need to Know About LED Safety Flares

June 19, 2020

Did you know that almost 1.35 million road accidents are happening every year? Most of them happen in low-income countries, but thousands of accidents take place in the United States too. The economic impact of US road crashes amounts to nearly $900 billion annually. 

Luckily, not all road accidents are serious. Most of them are minor, but still require drivers to put up signal flares on the road while they exchange phone numbers and insurance details. The emergency LED flare is necessary to alert other drivers that an accident took place, so that they can slow down. Keep reading to learn more about the LED safety flare, its purpose, and its advantages.

What Are Signal Flares?

You probably already know that the runways of most airports feature built-in lights or signal flares that are turned on at night. These flares help pilots land safely in low-light conditions. Such signal flares can also be used after a road accident to increase awareness and let other drivers know about the crash. Signal flares are usually used in conjunction with reflective triangles to gently divert traffic until the police or ambulance arrives.

What Types of Signal Flares Are Available?

For years, drivers used incendiary emergency flares. These are very cheap, easy to use, and they create a bright light that can be seen from a distance, even when fog is present. However, incendiary flares release a lot of heat and they can be dangerous if there are fuel puddles on the ground. In worst-case scenarios, they can start a fire and cause more damage and injuries to the cars and people involved in the accident.

You can also go for light sticks or chemical flares that burn for a limited amount of time. These might be useful in emergency situations, but remember that a chemical flare contains dangerous substances and it will release a lot of smoke. This type of smoke is toxic for you, your passengers, and other road participants, so it's best to simply avoid them to protect your health. 

That's why incendiary and chemical flares are slowly replaced by LED road flares. These flares are safer and they can be used over and over again. An incendiary flare can be used only once, so you will eventually have to buy more over the years. LED flares are reusable and they come with numerous advantages.

What Are the Advantages of LED Signal Flares?

LED stands for light-emitting diode and it's slowly replacing all types and formats of incandescent or incendiary lighting. You can buy LED light bulbs, LED strips, LED headlights for your car, etc. Here are the advantages of LED road flares and why you should be interested in them.

They Are Very Bright

LED flares can flash a bright light that can be seen from a very long distance. This is a good thing because other drivers will be alerted ahead of time and they will be able to slow down. These lights are also visible in different types of weather conditions and they have a very long lifespan.

On top of that, most LED road flares also come with different illumination patterns. For example, they can display the classic amber light, but they can also flash white light intermittently. You can choose what type of pattern to use for your LED road flares, depending on your preferences and road conditions.

Best of all, some LED road lights have a preinstalled and US Coast approved SOS code that will flash at the press of a button. The light will send a help message using traditional Morse codes to alert the authorities about your location. This can be very useful if you have been involved in an accident at night in a remote place and you have limited communication options with the police or paramedics.

They Are Waterproof and Crushproof

You might be happy to find out that LED lights that can be used as road flares are protected against the elements of nature. They can withstand different extreme weather conditions, so you can use them even when it rains or snows. LED signal flares are waterproof and dustproof. They are surrounded by a metal casing that protects the electronic components against dust, dirt, mud, debris, and other types of impurities.

But what if you accidentally run over a road flare with your car? Well, most of these LED lights will survive. The metal casing makes them crushproof and shatterproof, so you cannot damage them intentionally or unintentionally. You can even run them over with a truck and the light will still be reusable afterwards. As a result, LED road flares can be used for a long period of time and this saves you money because you don't have to buy more every year.

They Don't Catch Fire

As mentioned earlier, the problem with incendiary flares and other types of chemical road flares is that they might start a fire if they come in contact with fuel. You will not have this problem with LED light flares because these items do not release heat and they cannot start a fire if they come in contact with fuel or oil spilled on the road. They are a safe alternative for every responsible driver and they will eventually replace incendiary flares all over the world. 

They Are Rechargeable

Most types of electronics contain a lithium-ion rechargeable battery, including LED road flares. This battery is protected against harmful agents and it cannot be damaged by shocks, pressure, vibration, and water. The battery usually offers a long operation time of up to 8 hours in some cases. 

This means that you can take out the road flare, turn it on, and leave it on the road for hours while waiting for a towing vehicle or the police. When you arrive home, you can simply recharge the LED road light and use it again next time you have an emergency. Today's LED flares hold their power very well, so your signal light will be ready to use even after months of inactivity.

Some of Them Have a Magnetic Base

You might be happy to find out that some road flares come with a magnet attached to their base. This helps you stick them to your vehicle whenever necessary. For example, if you have been involved in an accident right after a road corner, you can stick the LED light at the highest point of your vehicle to make it visible for everyone from a distance. As a result, incoming traffic will see the light and slow down on time.

They Have a 360-Degree Light Output

Unlike headlights, LED flares are designed to spread the light in all directions. They act as a beacon, having a 360-degree light output, so you don't have to position the flare in a certain direction to make the light visible. This means that you can see the LED flare regardless of your location on the road and distance from the accident scene. 

Some of Them Are Suitable for Marine Application

A few LED flares are designed to be used in various circumstances, not only after road accidents. For example, some of them can be attached to a boat at night, making it easier for other people to locate its position. Best of all, these lights are waterproof and they can even float. This can be very useful if you want to create a perimeter around a damaged boat in low-light conditions, helping the authorities find you quicker.

They Have a Long Lifespan

LED technology is very advanced and it makes different everyday products and items last much longer than conventional light bulbs. For example, a LED flare can have a shelf life of at least 10 years and the light can be used for up to 100,000 continuous hours. This means that you don't have to buy additional signal flares over the years and save a lot of money as a result.

They Are Lightweight and Easy to Store

LED flares are not cumbersome products that will occupy a lot of place in your garage or car trunk. They actually weigh very little and can be easily stored one on top of the other if necessary. Some LED flares are equipped with a small hanger, allowing you to attach them to a tree branch. This can be useful if your car is not very tall and you need to raise awareness on the scene of the accident from a distance.

Now You Know More About LED Signal Flares

As you can see, LED signal flares offer so many advantages over conventional lights, not to mention that they also come at reasonable prices. Buying just a few of these lights can take one worry off your shoulders for at least a decade, making you a safer driver and saving you money.
